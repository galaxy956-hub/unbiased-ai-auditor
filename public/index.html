The Problem We're Solving

Hidden Bias Has Real-World Consequences

When AI systems learn from historically biased data, they encode and amplify those exact same discriminatory patterns. A hiring algorithm trained on past data may systematically reject qualified women. A loan model may deny credit to minority applicants at twice the rate of equivalent white applicants. A healthcare risk scorer may under-prioritize Black patients' care needs.

Without rigorous, accessible fairness tooling, these harms remain invisible — until someone sues, a regulator fines, or a life is irreversibly affected.
